  • What is a heading

What is a Heading?

A Heading is a type of element to define a title or subtitle you want to display on your webpage.

Headings are important because they identify the structure and content of your webpage.

Headings are also good for:

  • Accessibility
  • Improved User Experience/Readability
  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO) - making your website easier to find by Search Engines
    • Headings serve as keywords that can help improve a site's ranking on Google or another search engine.

Different Types of Headings

There are six levels of headings, each ranked by importance. The <h1> tag represents the main title of the page, while <h6> is the least important.

These levels form a hierarchy, similar to an outline that organizes your content.

<h1> is typically used for the main page title and should only appear once.

<h2> introduces the main sections of the page.

<h3> is used for subsections within those sections.

Lower-level tags such as <h4>, <h5>, and <h6> are used for smaller details and are less common.

IMPORTANT: DON’T JUMP HEADINGS!!!!

Why???

  1. It breaks page structure making it look disorganized in the long run.
  2. It can cause Accessibility Issues.
      Screen readers rely on headings being in order to understand the structure of the page. If you skip levels, it can confuse them. It also breaks the natural structure of the page and makes it harder for users to quickly scan and understand the content.
  3. It Hurts Readability.
      Headings help users quickly scan and understand the structure of a page. If they are used incorrectly or out of order, the content becomes harder to follow.

How to make headings:

              <h1>HEADING 1</h1>
              <h2>HEADING 2</h2>
              <h3>HEADING 3</h3>
              <h4>HEADING 4</h4>
              <h5>HEADING 5</h5>
              <h6>HEADING 6</h6>
